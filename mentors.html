Mentors

Welcome to our esteemed mentor network! Our hackathon has attracted a global roster of experts from academia, industry, and NGOs, each bringing unique insights to the intersection of Generative AI and Gender issues. These mentors, with their diverse backgrounds and specializations, are here to guide and inspire you throughout your journey. Explore the profiles below to discover the wealth of knowledge and experience our mentors bring to the table. Get ready to learn, grow, and create groundbreaking solutions with world-class support!

Emma Pickering

Head of Tech & Economic Abuse at Refuge

Expertise Areas: Safety, Security, Privacy Features, Tech and Gender Intersection, AI Risks

Emma Pickering is the Head of Tech & Economic Abuse at Refuge, where she plays a pivotal role in preventing technology-facilitated domestic abuse. With a background in working with young people and services, she has developed expertise in tech and economic abuse, providing direct support to women and children facing these challenges. Emma also manages external partnerships and stakeholders, collaborates closely with Refuge's policy and fundraising teams, and contributes to legislative improvements for survivors of technology-facilitated abuse.

Hanae Bezad

Impact Entrepreneur & Author

Expertise Areas: Ideation, AI for reproductive health

Hanae Bezad is an impact entrepreneur, international project manager, board advisor, curator, and the author of Being Other, a personal reflection on culture, gender, and identity. Her expertise spans government and international development institutions, civil society, and business. At the heart of her work lies a keen interest in the convergence of technology, sustainability, inclusion, entrepreneurship, and international development, and supporting the rise of a creative feminine leadership. Born and raised in Morocco, Hanae has worked and traveled across Europe, Africa, and the Americas and Asia, developing and nurturing impact tech enabled creative ventures with different communities and organizations. She is currently focused on nurturing the burgeoning leadership movement among Black, Indigenous, and Women of Color (BIWOC), supporting emerging entrepreneurs and philanthropists, and fostering cultural innovators who are driving global impact.

Jyothsna Belliappa, PhD

Faculty Member, Srishti Manipal Institute of Art, Design & Technology, Manipal Academy of Higher Education

Expertise Areas: Gender, work, personal life, gender in IT industry, in teaching and higher education, intersection of gender with age, disabiliy, and minority position

Jyothsna brings a wealth of experience as an educator, researcher, and writer. With a PhD in Women's Studies from the University of York, UK, she has taught at prestigious institutions including The University of York and Azim Premji University. Her research interests span teachers and teaching, the IT industry, identity and self, and the interplay between work and personal life. Her book "Gender, Class and Reflexive Modernity in India" which discusses how middle class women create a sense of self by drawing on multiple discourses prevalent within contemporary India was published by Palgrave Macmillan, UK. A strong supporter of participatory methods of teaching, Jyothsna facilitates professional development workshops and mentors research scholars and teachers across primary and secondary education. She teaches at Srishti Manipal Institute of Art, Design and Technology, Bengaluru.

Loganathan Ramasamy, PhD

AI/ML Tech Lead at MSD, Prague

Expertise Areas: Natural Language Processing, Generative AI, Retrieval Augmented Generation, Low Resource Languages

Loganathan's overall expertise lie in natural language processing (NLP) and related subtasks in NLP. Major part of his research work thus far focused on working with low-resource languages and corpus linguistics. He has experience working in most of the standard NLP tasks. During his PhD at Charles University (Prague), he worked on one of the most pertinent problem facing corpus-based or data-oriented dependency parsing, i.e., how to parse a low-resource natural language that either has no annotated dependency treebank or has little other resources such as part-of-speech (POS) taggers. When it comes to NLP concerning low-resource languages, he is inclined to apply semi-supervised and cross-lingual approaches.
